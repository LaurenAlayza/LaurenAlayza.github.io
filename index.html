About Me

Hey there!


My name is Lauren. I am an aspiring full stack web developer. My coding journey began in May 2020 when I joined Eleven Fifty Academy, a six-month part-time coding bootcamp. Prior to joining EFA as a student, I recieved my BA in Political Science from Indiana University in 2017.

As a clasically trained cellist and performer, I find myself utilizing the self-discipline of a musician to practice, practice, practice in order to work towards mastery of programming languages. As a bilingual, I find myself using similar learning techniques from learning a language in my journey to becoming a better programmer. As a political theory enthusiast, advocate and community organizer, I am drawn to imagine the infinite possibilities when combining government and technology.


In my current role, I am a Technical Sales Representative at Schneider Geospatial. In this role I get the opportuniy to partner with local government leaders to help them bridge the gap with geographic information system solutions through software-as-a-service. Last summer I made a career pivot towards software sales by participating in TechPoint's Software Sales Bootcamp. I made a pivot to software sales to better understand the different departments that make tech companies successful.
